Welcome

Elissa Day

Undergraduate studying Egyptology with Chinese

Contact Me

Elissa, who attends the University of Liverpool, will be the first ever student to graduate from the institution with a degree in Egyptology with Chinese, which includes the study of hieroglyphs, Egyptian history, archaeology and literature, as well as Mandarin. Since starting university, Elissa has taken up fencing, and runs her own radio show, having also appeared on The 3rd Degree (BBC Radio 4) earlier this year. She works as a Student Advocate, helping to run Widening Participation & Outreach projects for the university. In addition, she received a government-funded place to travel to Shanghai and study at East China Normal University for three weeks at Easter. Over summer, she will be working at an international summer school in York, an Egyptological charity, and a corporate law firm. Elissa is an ex-SEO Scholar who is now on two SEO corporate law programmes, and hopes to become a solicitor after graduation.

Who? What? Where? Why? When? How?

Who? Hi there. I'm Elissa, a 1st year university student studying Egyptology with Chinese.

What? Yep, Egyptology with Chinese. An odd sounding combination, but one that makes a lot more sense than you think. Additionally, I work as a Student Advocate and am a member of three societies - fencing, radio and SACE (Society of Archaeology, Classics and Egyptology).

Where? I'm currently a student at the University of Liverpool, but I am from London and am based there during the holidays.

Why? I know how hard it is for students these days to get the experience they need for the graduate market. Last summer, I was determined to work in order to save up for university. I sent off countless applications, with an interview rate of around 20%. Most employers weren't willing to take me on for just a few months, but my perseverance paid off, with not one, but two jobs, at Clinton Cards and Build-A-Bear Workshop.
