BIOGRAPHY

2016

After leaving secondary school, I worked during the summer before I started university, studying for a BA in Egyptology with Chinese at the University of Liverpool.

2017

I studied abroad at ECNU (East China Normal University) in Shanghai, as part of the British Council's Study China programme, where I achieved a Distinction.

At the end of the academic year, I was elected as President of the Society of Archaeology, Classics and Egyptology, a role which I held until May 2019.

I also began hosting a weekly radio show on the University of Liverpool's station, LSRadio. I then appeared on the BBC Radio 4 quiz show, 'The 3rd Degree', as the student contestant for Egyptology.

2018

I began working as a Career Coach for the University's brand new Career Studio, where I was part of a team of students, supported by full-time career professionals, responsible for checking CVs, advising students on job applications, and assisting with careers events throughout the year.

2019

I graduated in the summer of 2019, as the first student at the University of Liverpool, and possibly the world, with a BA in Egyptology with Chinese (1st Class).

I travelled to China with a group of academic staff, and visited Northeast Normal University in Changchun, where I gave a presentation on student life at the University of Liverpool, to an audience of postgraduate students.

After an internship in the Delivery Team at Auto Trader, I began my full-time role as a Graduate Intern in the Careers and Employability Service at the University of Liverpool.

2020

I started the year with a trip to Sudan, where I visited a number of sites, including the stunning pyramids of Meroe.

Since October 2020, I have been studying for an MPhil in Egyptology at the University of Cambridge.

2021

In January, I was one of three gamers featured in a new BBC Three documentary, 'Gaming and Me: Connections, Identity, and Support'. I talked about Animal Crossing, and its positive impact on mental health.
